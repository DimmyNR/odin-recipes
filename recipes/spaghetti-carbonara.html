Spaghetti Carbonara

Description:

A classic pasta dish that is easily recognisable across the globe. This dish is also fairly easy to make and will not take long to prepare at all.

Ingredients

  • 100g pancetta
  • 50g pecorino cheese
  • 50g parmesan
  • 3 large eggs
  • 350g spaghetti
  • 2 plump garlic cloves, peeled and left whole
  • 50g unsalted butter
  • sea salt and freshly ground black pepper

Steps

  1. Bring a large saucepan of water to a boil
  2. Chop up the 100g of pancetta, after removing the rind. Finely grate 50g of pecorino cheese and 50g parmesan and mix them together.
  3. Beat 3 large eggs in a medium bown and season it with some freshly grated black pepper. Set everything aside.
  4. Add 1 teaspoon of salt to the boiling water, add 350g of spaghetti and when the water comes to a boil, cook at a constant simmer for 10 minutes.
  5. Squash 2 peeled plump garlic cloves with the side of a knife.
  6. While the spaghetti is cooking, fry the pancetta and garlic. Drop 50g of unsalted butter into a large frying pan or wok and tip the pancetta/garlic after the butter has melted.
  7. Leave to cook on a medium heat for about 5 minutes while stirring, until the pancetta becomes gold and crisp. The garlic should have diffused its flavour by now, so remove it with a spoon and discard.
  8. Keep the heat low for the pancetta. Once the pasta is ready, lift from the water and put into the frying pan with the pancetta (Don't chuck out the pasta water yet).
  9. Mix most of the cheese with the eggs (while keeping a little bit for later).
  10. Take the pan of spaghetti and pancetta off the heat. Then, pour in the eggs and cheese. Using tongs, lift the spaghetti so it mixes with the egg cheese mixture.
  11. Add a few tablespoons of the pasta cooking water in order to keep it saucy until it is moist but not wet.
  12. Serve onto a plate or bowl and sprinkle the remaining cheese and some black pepper.
